The spreadsheet (medical_srs_v2.xlsx) has 4 sheets:
The lesson-database is the engine — all 90 of your lessons are pre-loaded. You only ever touch two blue columns: Last Review (Ctrl+; for today's date) and Mastery (0-5). Everything else — interval, next review date, status, priority — auto-calculates via formulas. The Dashboard gives you a live command center: overdue count, today's reviews, days until your May 12 exam, progress by module, mastery distribution. The Weekly tab forecasts your next 7 days of workload so you can see overload days coming. The How-To tab is a minimal cheat sheet.
The AppScript (appscript_v2.js) adds three critical features your v1 was missing:
First, color-coded calendar events — mastery 0 shows red, mastery 3 shows cyan, mastery 5 shows purple. You can glance at your calendar and instantly see where your weak spots are. Second, Smart Reschedule — when you've been away for days and have 40 overdue items, instead of panicking, run this from the menu. It spreads overdue reviews across the next 3 days with a cap of 15/day. Third, Daily Digest — one click shows exactly what to study today, sorted by priority.
The key design insight: your old system punished you for missing days (everything became "STUDY NOW" with no differentiation). This one recovers gracefully — overdue items are clearly marked and prioritized, but the system doesn't collapse. When you review an overdue item, just set mastery based on actual recall. If you forgot it, mastery 0 reschedules it for tomorrow. If you still remember, mastery 2 pushes it out a week. The system self-heals.
To set this up: import the xlsx into Google Sheets, paste the AppScript into Extensions → Apps Script, run setupTriggers() once, and you're done.
Additional tools worth considering for your exam prep — since you have 90 lessons and 45 days, you might want to pair this with Anki for active recall flashcards on the hardest topics (it uses a more granular SRS algorithm), and use the Pomodoro technique (25 min study / 5 min rest) to maintain focus during review sessions.
